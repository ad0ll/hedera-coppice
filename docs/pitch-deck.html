The Problem

Green Bonds Have a Trust Crisis

Greenwashing Risk

Issuers self-certify bonds as "green" with minimal external verification. The EU Green Bond Standard exists (effective Dec 2024), but only 3 issuers have adopted it.

[3][4]

Opaque Fund Tracking

Use-of-proceeds reports arrive 12-18 months late as static PDFs. Investors have no real-time visibility into whether funds actually reach green projects.

Compliance Fragmentation

KYC/AML and jurisdiction checks happen off-chain in spreadsheets. Transfer restrictions are voluntary, not protocol-level. No on-chain enforcement.

The Solution

Institutional-Grade Green Bond Compliance on a Carbon-Negative Chain

Coppice tokenizes green bonds using ERC-3643 ($32B+ tokenized [6]) on Hedera (carbon-negative, verified by UCL [7]).

Protocol-Level Compliance

ERC-3643 enforces identity verification, KYC/AML claims, and jurisdiction checks in the token contract itself. Non-compliant transfers revert on-chain. Not optional. Not a wrapper.

Smart Contracts (Hedera EVM)

Immutable Audit Trail

Every mint, transfer, freeze, and pause is logged to an append-only HCS topic. Regulators, auditors, and investors all verify the same immutable record.

Hedera Consensus Service

On-Chain Use-of-Proceeds

Fund allocations to green projects are recorded to HCS with category, amount, and timestamp. No more waiting for annual PDF reports. Verifiable in real time.

Hedera Consensus Service

Stablecoin Settlement

Bond purchases settle in eUSD, a native HTS fungible token accessible as an ERC-20 via HIP-218 long-zero address. Native multi-service integration.

Hedera Token Service

What this is NOT: A wrapper around an ERC-20 with optional compliance. ERC-3643 bakes compliance into the transfer function. You physically cannot send tokens to a non-compliant address.

Why Hedera

The Right Chain for Green Finance

1. Carbon-Negative Network

Verified by UCL's Centre for Blockchain Technologies. Energy per transaction: 0.000003 kWh — less than a Google search. Carbon offsets via Terrapass green-e certified credits. [7]

A green bond should run on the greenest network.

2. Low, Predictable Fees

ERC-3643 transfers trigger 4-6 contract calls (identity, claims, country, compliance modules). At ~$0.0001/tx on Hedera vs. $5-50 on Ethereum L1, compliance-heavy tokens are economically viable. [8]

3. Strategic Alignment

Hedera Foundation joined the ERC-3643 Association (March 2025) alongside DTCC, ABN AMRO, Deloitte, Fireblocks, OpenZeppelin. ERC-3643 integrated into Asset Tokenization Studio (Nov 2025). [9][10]

4. Multi-Service Architecture

No other chain combines: EVM smart contracts + Consensus Service (audit) + Token Service (settlement) + Mirror Node (queries). Coppice is a native multi-service Hedera app, not just EVM code on a relay.

Key Learnings

ERC-3643 on Hedera works. T-REX v4.1.6 deploys and runs correctly on Hedera EVM. No modifications needed.

HCS is ideal for compliance audit trails. Immutable, timestamped, publicly queryable — exactly what regulators want.

HTS + EVM interop is powerful. HIP-218 long-zero mapping lets HTS tokens behave as ERC-20s inside EVM contracts.

Solo scope demands focus. Every feature is tested and functional. No half-built features.
